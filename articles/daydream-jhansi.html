daydream jhansi

what happens when you give teenagers 24 hours, free food, and permission to build whatever game they want?

i wanted to find out.

so i organized daydream jhansi — a 24-hour game jam where students aged 13–18 came together to build a game from scratch in just one day.

over the course of 24 hours, participants formed teams, learned new tools, and shipped real games. many of them were building something like this for the first time.

to keep the event accessible, daydream jhansi was completely free. every participant received free food and unlimited snacks throughout the event, which helped keep the energy high and the environment collaborative.

anyone who built and published their game on itch.io received stickers and merch. we also ran community voting and awarded prizes for the best games.

all games created during the jam were made open source and uploaded to itch.io, so anyone can play them or contribute further.

one of my favorite projects was built by a 16-year-old participant:

  • itch.io: https://lnkd.in/g3rYdz_X
  • github: https://lnkd.in/gGT6A7Ap

the event received 150+ signups, far beyond what we initially expected.

to fund it, i personally reached out to builders and organizations i admired. after cold messaging the CTO of Zerodha, we secured sponsorship support.

in total, we raised $2,000 USD in prize money, with support from FOSS United, Hack Club, Z Fellows, and others.

daydream jhansi showed me how much untapped talent exists when young people are given space, trust, and the right environment to build.

many participants shipped their first real project that day — and that mattered more than any prize.
